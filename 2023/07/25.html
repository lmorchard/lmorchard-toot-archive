So I read that WaPo story on mastodon being "rife" with child porn. Then I read the Stanford paper. My take: Ignore the story, but do read the paper.

On one hand, roughly 0.03% of 325000 public posts found to be child porn over 2 days doesn't sound "rife". And, to be fair, the paper does not describe the situation as such.

On the other hand, that's still too much. Like an industrially-acceptable proportion of rat turds in my morning oatmeal.

The paper also has some good discussion on how the fediverse could sorely use some cross-instance shared moderation and automated detection systems - and how, in particular, federation makes it easy to overwhelm existing detection systems.

We need better defense systems that don't rely on individual admins doing their overworked best
